# Dissertator

You know the feeling. A hundred PDFs scattered across folders. A spreadsheet you half-read three weeks ago. A brilliant note scribbled somewhere you'll never find again. And a blank page, waiting.

**Dissertator is a quiet, patient companion for the long work of turning a pile of sources into a finished report, paper, or brief.**

## What it holds for you

Dissertator keeps three things together, finally side by side:

- **Your sources** — PDFs, spreadsheets, notes, even photographs of old printed reports. Drop in a messy folder, and Dissertator quietly reads every page, so nothing stays buried or forgotten.
- **Your work** — the drafts and writing-in-progress that grow as your thinking grows.
- **Your thoughts** — the highlights, threads, and half-formed ideas always threatening to slip away.

As you write, the right passage surfaces at just the right moment — the statistic, the quote, the page you meant to come back to. You stay grounded in evidence. You never lose a citation. And your drafts live safely beside the very sources they were built on.

## It thinks with you. It does not think for you.

This is the part that matters most. Dissertator will not write your report. It remembers where everything is, surfaces the passage you need, and clears the path — so *you* can walk it. Your judgment stays yours. Your voice stays yours. Your argument stays yours. Dissertator simply makes sure that nothing you've already read gets lost along the way.

It is a thinking partner, not a ghostwriter — and it keeps a gentle little list of what still needs your attention: *these scanned pages are ready for you*, *this draft is ready to grow*, *this source hasn't been read yet*.

## Who it's for

- **Researchers and graduate students** carrying the weight of a thesis or dissertation.
- **Writers of social, NGO, and government reports** — people who turn scattered field documents into something credible.
- **Policy analysts** who need every claim traceable to a source.
- **Anyone** doing evidence-based writing across many documents at once.

It won't do the hard part for you — the reading, the judging, the deciding what it all means. But it will hold the rest, so you have both hands free for that part. The whole point is to help you *finish*.
